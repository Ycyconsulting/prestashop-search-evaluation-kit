## Hard-gate evidence

### Visibility and data boundary

The inactive, visibility-`none` telescope remained absent. The exercised search, filter, product, currency, and add-to-cart path stayed on the private shop/module origin and sent no query, catalogue, cart, or order fields to Neuroplugin.

The browser also requested three malformed `http://modules/blockreassurance/...` SVG paths generated by the preinstalled `blockreassurance` module. They were not NP Search requests and carried no search, query, cart, or order data. This observation is disclosed rather than silently excluded; it is not an audit of every installed module or every storefront action.

### Index correctness

The incremental fixture mutation changed name, price, stock, and category together. The changed product appeared once in its new state and disappeared from the old exact term. A full rebuild left 25 indexed products and the deterministic fixture intact. A safe product-index clear made the target unsearchable, and the documented single-product recovery restored exactly one result.

The full rebuild took 85–95 ms in repeated tiny-fixture checks with a 25,165,824-byte observed PHP peak. These are smoke observations, not catalogue-scale or latency guarantees.

### Storefront continuity

Exact product and combination references preserved the intended identity. `NPBENCH-TEE-L` resolved attribute 43, Large/Red, and its out-of-stock state instead of opening the default combination. Small/Blue remained selectable and addable. Product, variant, price, stock, currency, and cart state stayed consistent; the exercised two-item USD cart total was `$117.89`.

### Rollback

Uninstall removed the checked NP Search tables, configuration, and resources, and native search resumed. A clean install of the same 2.13.4 ZIP then passed again. This exercises the rollback gate; it does not promise zero operational work on a differently customized shop.

## Limitations

- The checklist result is maintainer-run synthetic evidence, not independent merchant evidence.
- Only English was exercised in the primary 18-row run.
- Classic was the primary theme. A custom theme or search/facet override requires staging validation.
- Timings and memory figures describe a tiny local fixture, not production scale.
- The report establishes observed behavior of one exact package; it does not establish causal revenue lift, universal compatibility, legal compliance, or external email/payment delivery.
- A second-model review can challenge implementation details, but it is not a substitute for an independent store run. No such review is counted in the 17/0/0/1 score.
